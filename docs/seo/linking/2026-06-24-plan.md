post is grounded in the real spec — no invention required.

## 3. Orphan pages (no incoming internal links once clusters are built)

Today (pre-build):
- **`/about`** — reachable only from global nav + footer. Acceptable for a bio page; leave it,
  but it should NOT receive cluster links (it is off-topic; linking to it would dilute clusters).
- **`/download`** — intentionally orphaned / transactional (post-checkout). Keep noindex; fine.

Post-build orphan risks to watch:
- Any new spoke that ships before its pillar links down to it → **temporary orphan**. Rule:
  never publish a spoke until (a) it links up to its pillar AND (b) the pillar links back down.
- `/self-demo` if promoted (see §4) would be orphaned unless `/#agents` and `/skill` link to it.

There are currently **no true content orphans** — but there is the opposite problem: **almost no
internal links carry topical anchor text**, so equity pools on `/` and `/skill` and nothing deep
exists to receive it.

## 4. Promote on-page sections → standalone indexable routes

The single biggest ranking lever: `/#what`, `/#agents`, `/#self-demo` are real, substantive
content that today can only rank as part of `/`. Splitting the strongest ones into their own
routes gives Google distinct URLs to rank, gives the clusters real pillars, and creates link
targets. Recommendation (impact + effort on 1-5):

| Section (today) | Promote to route | Becomes | Impact | Effort | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `/#what` (WhatIsWebMCP) | `/what-is-webmcp` | **Pillar A** | 5 | 2 | Highest-volume head term; section copy already strong. Keep a short teaser on `/` that links out. |
| `/#agents` (AgentInstructions) | `/implement-webmcp` | **Pillar B** | 5 | 3 | Reframe protocol steps into a build guide; commercial intent; links down to Skill. |
| `/#self-demo` (SelfDemo console) | `/self-demo` | Interactive demo page | 3 | 2 | Linkable proof page; strong for "WebMCP demo / try WebMCP". |
| `/#skill` (SkillFeatures) | keep on `/`, mirror on `/skill` | — | 2 | 1 | `/skill` already the product/pillar-C page; don't fragment. |
| `/#pricing` (Pricing) | keep on `/` | — | 1 | 1 | Transactional; fragment is fine, no SEO need. |
| DemoSection | keep on `/` | — | 1 | 1 | Supporting visual; no standalone value. |

> Pattern: when a section is promoted, leave a 2-3 sentence teaser in place on `/` with a
> descriptive in-body link to the new route ("Full explainer: what is WebMCP →"). This preserves
> the home page's narrative while creating the indexable pillar.

## 6. Anti-pattern compliance check
- ✅ ≤10-15 in-body links per page (pillars link to ≤9 spokes + 1 cross-pillar; well under cap).
- ✅ No identical anchor text reused across two source pages (anchors varied per row above).
- ✅ No links between unrelated clusters except the deliberate one-way B→C bridges.
- ✅ No "click here" anchors — all anchors are descriptive + keyword-bearing.
- ✅ No circular over-linking: pillar triangle is A→B→C→A; cross-spoke bridges are one-way only.

## 7. Payload / future-CMS note
This is a Next.js (not Payload) site, so the cluster graph here lives in JSX/MDX, not a Payload
`relatedPosts` relationship field. When/if blog content moves to a CMS, model the spoke→pillar
and cross-spoke links as a hasMany relationship field rather than inline `<a>` tags, so the graph
stays queryable and consistent — same principle, different substrate.
